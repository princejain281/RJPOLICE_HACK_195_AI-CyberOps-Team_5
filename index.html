Phishing is a cyber attack method where deceptive emails, messages, or websites impersonate trusted entities to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information like passwords or financial details. Attackers exploit human trust, often using urgency or fear to prompt quick responses. Common forms include email phishing, spear phishing (targeted), vishing (voice), and smishing (SMS). Vigilance, verification, and security measures like two-factor authentication are crucial to thwart phishing attempts and protect against unauthorized access or data breaches.

Spam URLs are web links distributed through unsolicited messages, emails, or online platforms with the intent to deceive or manipulate users. These URLs often lead to malicious websites, phishing pages, or sites aiming to spread malware. The goal is typically to collect sensitive information, such as login credentials or financial details, or to compromise the user's device. Effective spam URL detection and avoidance are essential for maintaining online security, requiring users to exercise caution, use security tools, and stay informed about common tactics employed by cybercriminals to safeguard against potential threats.
